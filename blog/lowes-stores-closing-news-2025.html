Lowe's to close stores for 24 hours at all 1,700 locations

  • 08 April 2025
  • By WIF Group

Lowe's Stores Closing News 2025: Why It’s Happening, When, and What It Means

In recent days, the internet has been buzzing with the Lowe's stores closing news, prompting confusion and concern among customers. Headlines like “Lowe's to shut all 1,700 U.S. stores” caused many to believe that the home improvement giant was permanently going out of business. But the reality is much more measured and, in fact, a sign of goodwill rather than financial instability. Let’s break down what’s actually happening, why Lowe's made this decision, when it’s taking place, and how it fits into the bigger picture of their company culture and operations..

📅 When Are Lowe's Stores Closing in 2025?

Lowe’s has announced that all 1,700+ of its U.S. store locations will be closed on Sunday, April 20, 2025, in observance of Easter Sunday. This is not a permanent closure or a sign of bankruptcy. It’s a planned, temporary one-day shutdown meant to give employees a chance to spend the holiday with their families.

This means all stores will resume their regular operations on Monday, April 21, 2025.

🔍 Why Is Lowe's Closing Stores for a Day?

The decision to close on Easter Sunday is part of Lowe’s long-standing tradition of valuing family, rest, and employee well-being. This move aligns with how Lowe’s has historically closed on major holidays like Thanksgiving and Christmas.
According to company spokespeople, this decision is a “thank you” gesture to store associates, acknowledging their hard work and dedication throughout the year. After a busy season of spring home improvement, this holiday break offers employees much-needed rest and time with loved ones.

💬A Lowe’s representative stated:

“We understand the importance of time with family, especially during the holidays. This 24-hour closure is a way to show appreciation for our team members' dedication and to reinforce our company’s core values.” This emphasis on people-first policy is becoming increasingly important in a retail world that often puts profits over people

Official Statement From Lowe’s.

🧠 What Is the Real Reason Behind This Move?

Contrary to speculation and rumors, the real reason for the temporary closure is employee wellness—not poor sales, bankruptcy, or downsizing. In fact:

  • There are no financial red flags suggesting that Lowe’s is struggling.
  • No layoffs or long-term closures have been announced.
  • The company continues to operate healthily, serving millions of customers across North America.

    • It’s simply a strategic, compassionate decision that mirrors their approach during Thanksgiving and Christmas in past years.

    📊 How Does Lowe’s Compare With Competitors?

    Interestingly, Lowe’s decision sets them apart from competitors like The Home Depot, which plans to remain open on Easter Sunday in many locations. While this might give competitors a short-term sales edge, Lowe's is playing the long game—building loyalty through values-driven decisions.

    📍 Is This Closure Nationwide?

    Yes, the closure affects all 1,700+ U.S. stores, including:

  • Lowe’s Home Improvement centers
  • Lowe’s Outlet Stores
  • Regional warehouses and distribution centers.

    • Canadian stores may operate differently based on local regulations and customs, so customers in Canada should check local hours.

    🧭 How Customers Can Plan Ahead

    To avoid inconvenience, customers should:

  • Shop ahead of Easter Sunday, especially for spring or home improvement needs
  • Use Lowe’s online store, which may still offer browsing and customer service options even if in-store shopping is paused.
  • Plan pickups and deliveries either before or after April 20.

    • 🛠️ Is This a New Trend for Retailers?

    More retailers are beginning to adopt this model, prioritizing employee wellness over profits—especially on holidays. It may become a growing trend, especially as customer expectations shift towards socially responsible companies.

    ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

    1. Is Lowe’s permanently closing stores in 2025?
    No, Lowe's is only closing temporarily on Easter Sunday, April 20, 2025.

    2. Why is Lowe’s closed on Easter Sunday?
    To give employees a break to spend time with their families and recognize their contributions.

    3. Will online orders be affected during the closure?
    While stores will be closed, the website may still be available for browsing and placing orders.

    4. Is Lowe’s going out of business?
    Absolutely not. The company is financially stable and continues to grow.

    5. Are any Lowe’s locations closing permanently?
    There are no announcements or indications of permanent closures as of now.

    6. Will this be an annual closure going forward?
    It’s likely, as Lowe’s has made similar moves on other major holidays in the past.

    🎯 Conclusion: What This Means for Lowe's and Its Customers

    The 2025 Lowe's stores closing news may have caused a stir, but the reality is positive: it’s a heartwarming decision to honor employees and reinforce company values. Far from being a sign of decline, this move underscores Lowe’s commitment to its workforce and community.
    So, rest assured—Lowe’s isn’t going anywhere. They're just taking one meaningful day off to say, “Thank you.”
